## Partnership Model

Building an integration with Ramp is a partnership focused on delivering a high-quality experience to shared customers. Each side plays a clear role.

**Your responsibilities:**
- **Pricing and monetization** — You set your pricing, bill customers directly, and keep 100% of revenue. Ramp does not process payments or participate in billing.
- **Building and hosting** — You develop and maintain your integration using Ramp's APIs.
- **Customer support** — You assist customers through your own support channels and provide a public support email.
- **Documentation** — You host setup guides, workflow instructions, and troubleshooting on your site.
- **Marketing assets** — You supply the logo, descriptions, screenshots, and videos for your listing.

**Ramp's responsibilities:**
- **API troubleshooting & advisory** — Ramp helps you debug API issues and offers guidance on best practices.
- **Product review and guidance** — Ramp reviews your integration flows and provides feedback. Approval is required before production access.
- **Distribution** — Your integration reaches 50,000+ businesses once approved.

## Phases

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1: Build">
    Build your integration in the Ramp Sandbox environment. Use the [Sandbox guide](/guides/sandbox) to get started with demo data and test flows.

    Key checklist items:
    - [ ] Create your app in the [Developer Console](https://app.ramp.com/developer)
    - [ ] Implement OAuth and required scopes for your use case
    - [ ] Build and test your core integration workflows end-to-end
    - [ ] Set up webhooks for real-time event handling
    - [ ] Implement idempotency and retry logic
    - [ ] Write customer-facing documentation (publicly hosted)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2: Apply for Production Access">
    Once your Sandbox integration is complete, apply for production access.

    Required materials:
    - [ ] Security disclosures (see Security Requirements below)
    - [ ] Public support email address
    - [ ] Customer-facing documentation URL
    - [ ] Description of your integration's data flows

    [Submit a Developer API support ticket](https://ramp.com/developer-api/support) to begin the production access review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3: Beta Testing">
    After production access is granted, test your integration with a small cohort of real customers before a broader launch.

    - [ ] Validate end-to-end flows with production data
    - [ ] Confirm support processes are in place
    - [ ] Gather feedback and iterate
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 4: Apply for a Public Listing">
    Once your integration is production-tested and stable, apply for a public listing on the Ramp Integrations page.

    Required materials:
    - [ ] Logo (square format, high resolution)
    - [ ] Short description (1–2 sentences)
    - [ ] Long description (feature highlights, use cases)
    - [ ] Screenshots or demo video
    - [ ] Setup documentation URL
    - [ ] Internal support guide for Ramp teams
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pricing

You control your pricing. Ramp does not charge fees or take a revenue share. Common pricing models include:

- Flat annual subscription
- Implementation fee plus annual renewal
- Usage-based pricing (e.g., per transaction or per bill)
- Tiered feature bundles based on functionality

**Best practices:**
- Price relative to the value your integration delivers to customers
- Keep pricing predictable and easy to understand
- Factor in implementation cost, ongoing maintenance, and support burden

## Documentation Requirements

High-quality documentation improves customer activation and reduces support load. It is also used by Ramp's sales, customer success, and support teams when discussing your integration.

Your documentation should:

- Be publicly accessible and hosted on your website
- Include prerequisites for both Ramp and your platform
- Provide step-by-step setup instructions
- Detail key user workflows and common use cases
- Offer troubleshooting tips and a FAQ section

<Tip>
  Ramp's systems may surface your documentation links when customers and internal teams ask about your integration — strong docs are one of your most effective marketing and support assets.
</Tip>

## Security Requirements

Partners must fully complete security disclosures and provide:

- Security certifications and/or attestations such as SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, PCI, or a recent third-party penetration test
- Full certification and/or pen test reports (not just summary letters)
- A public URL to your security policy
- A dedicated and monitored security contact email address
- Written descriptions covering:
  - What data your integration processes and how it is secured
  - Where and how data is stored
  - Your data retention policy
  - Whether and how employees at your company can access customer data

<Note>
  Early-stage companies may use a recent third-party pen test in place of full SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certifications, subject to Ramp's review.
</Note>

## Support Requirements

You own first-line support for your integration. Ramp will route customer questions about your integration to you.

- Provide a **public support email address** — this will be visible to customers
- Maintain clear **customer-facing documentation** for setup, key workflows, and troubleshooting
- Provide an **internal support guide for Ramp teams** that explains how to triage issues, what information to collect, and how to escalate
